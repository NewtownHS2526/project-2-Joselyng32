R&B music

R&B, or rhythm and blues, is a genre of popular music that originated in African-American communities in the 1940s, blending jazz, blues, and gospel with smooth rhythms and soulful vocals. It has evolved significantly, with contemporary R&B incorporating elements from pop, hip-hop, and electronic music. Key characteristics include its strong backbeat, emphasis on vocal performance, and lyrics that often focus on love and heartbreak.
